Breaking up my major relationship with the BBC: Update on BBC Sounds.: Update on BBC Sounds.

Published on Saturday, 5 July 2025.

Hello, I'm The Insider Sandrush. It's been a sometimes I haven't regularly listening to BBC Radio, because of their change on how we listen to their radio stations. Since then, I've break up my full relationship with the BBC.

What happened?

Somewhere, between February-March, the BBC announced that that they will restrict the BBC Sounds service only to listeners in the UK.

Many people, primarily expats, feel affected when the BBC announced it.

Throughout the time, the BBC makes changes to make people understand the change, until the decision is finalised.

What's now?

For now, the BBC Sounds app on mobile will read out this message.

"This app will close from July 21st for anyone living outside the UK. Use the new BBC App to listen to BBC podcasts, Radio 4 and the World Service. Other BBC stations will still be available."

Meanwhile, on the website, it still show the original message, first seen in February.

Until 21 July, you can visit the website to listen to podcasts, and listening to Radio 4, and World Service. Altough, it has caveats.

  1. Schedule variant for BBC World Service:

    Weirdly, when you want to listen to other variant of BBC World Service (example: BBC World Service East Asia) on the BBC Audio, you will get a 404 message.

  2. No dark mode option on bbc.com (applies to whole website as well):

    If you've previously use the BBC Sounds, it will turn on dark mode. However, if you're on BBC Audio, incl. BBC.com, you will not get one.

  3. Hard to navigate:

    If you've previously use the BBC Sounds, navigating BBC Audio makes you rewire your brain, once again.
