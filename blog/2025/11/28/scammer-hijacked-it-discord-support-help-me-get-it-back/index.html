I falled for this scam in Discord, my account got hijacked, the Discord Support helped me to get back my account, and tips on how to avoid it. (aka "Scammer hijacked it, the Discord Support help me get it back")

Published on Friday, 28 November 2025.

What happened, and how to prevent it.

Hi. Earlier this month, I have been contacted by an old friend of mine, only to realise that later on, my account are being used for scamming other people, targeting my friends.

What happened?

On 27th October 2025, I got a message from my old friend asking me if I ever purchased Discord Nitro, which later I absolutely replied no on 1st November 2025.

Image 1

Later on, I moved onto life and I thought that I'm literally going to solve a problem, but hold on: "Did I even do any violations??"

Only to realises that few weeks later (22 November 2025), my friend, Marsch, and Airsuz DM'd me in Bluesky asking me if my Discord got hacked, which I didn't know at that time that I was tricked.

Image 2

What you can do to prevent this from happening to you too.

  1. Analyse the message: This kind of trick is done by engineering it socially. Usually, the messages goes like this, from an exerpt.
    2. "Are you sure? Because I got hacked yesterday 'cause I clicked some shady Nitro link on Discord. Now, the hacker dropped like $136 on Nitro and boosters using my account. Thought it was you 'cause you got a similar pfp to the sketchy link dude."

    Followed by:

    "So the problem is, I accidentally filed tickets on your accounts for doing illegal purchase/scamming instead of someone else, and support responded on the ticket that your account will be suspended and your IP address will be affected too."

  2. Look out for any weirdness: If you're instructed to appeal a ban with an attached email being associated with an individual named "Jason Lee", immediately ignore. As an added evidence, they will show a 'fake' Discord certificate that looks like this.



  3. "I cannot login to my old account after letting the scammer change it. How can I get it back?": Simple, contact Discord Support, tell them what happened, and if you remember your User ID, give it to them, this makes the job easier to done on Discord's side.

  4. Common sense: Just as a reminder, Discord Support will, and would NEVER, EVER:
    • Asking you for money as a form of verification.
    • Asking you to change email as a form of verification..
    • Asking you delete official email from Discord as a form of verification.
    • Contacted you via Discord.

End of word / Conclusion

Immediately after I got back my account, I automatically contacted everyone that the person messaging you before that was a scammer. Since then, I have activated multi-factor authentication.

Thank you for reading, and sorry if I dumb enough.

~ Sand, 28 November 2025.
